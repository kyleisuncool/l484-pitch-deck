# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Investors being pitched by Poly Nakamoto (Labs 484 originator) in a live presentation context, followed by leave-behind reading. The audience is small — one to a few people in a room. They are evaluating a $2.5M ask over 5 years for a sovereign tech campus in East Austin. They are not conventional VC investors; the right investor understands builder communities, open source, and why physical infrastructure for the sovereign tech movement matters. The deck must work both as a live presentation tool (main slides only) and a leave-behind document (leave-behind slides, text-dense, read without a presenter).

## Product Purpose

An investor pitch deck for Labs 484 × ArcTec Center. Labs 484 is an open source R&D nonprofit running 14 active startups across 4 Austin locations. ArcTec Center (formerly Sapien Center) is their flagship campus — a sovereign tech hub housing coworking, a noderunner hardware store, an organic grocery, a food kitchen, tiny home lodging, and a private member association. The deck presents the organization's mission, current traction ($19k/month MRR), and funding ask to investors who can get behind a movement, not just a business model.

Success: the investor understands what ArcTec is, believes it's real and already in motion, and wants to continue the conversation.

## Brand Personality

Sovereign. Grounded. Inevitable.

The movement is already happening — out of self-direction, not a thesis. The deck should feel like you're being let in on something already underway, not pitched a concept. Not a startup seeking permission — an organization with momentum inviting participation.

Voice: direct, specific, non-apologetic. Never corporate. Never hype. Confidence without swagger.

## Anti-references

- **Standard VC pitch deck**: Slide 1 problem / Slide 2 solution / Slide 3 TAM. Clean white, San Francisco or Inter, blue accent. Signals "seeking approval from the establishment."
- **Web3 / crypto maximalist**: Neon on black, laser eyes energy, WAGMI culture. Signals the wrong tribe — this is sovereign tech, not crypto degens.
- **Tech startup SaaS aesthetic**: Notion-adjacent soft gradients on white. "We make teams more productive." Consumer-grade confidence.
- **Corporate consultancy**: McKinsey charts, bullet-heavy, institutional gray, serif-heavy. The opposite of what L484 is.

## Design Principles

1. **Already in motion.** The visual language should feel like documentation of something real, not a proposal for something hypothetical. Dark, atmospheric, render-grounded.
2. **Sovereign, not sleek.** Avoid the polished-startup aesthetic. The roughness of the builder community is a feature. Glassmorphism should feel like infrastructure, not decoration.
3. **Earn every word.** Copy density is a choice. Main slides: sparse, signal-dense. Leave-behinds: richer, but still no filler.
4. **Investor context, not investor grammar.** The deck does not follow conventional pitch structure to appease investor pattern-matching. It follows the L484 story.
5. **The presenter is the product.** Poly Nakamoto delivering this live is the primary medium. The slides are a backdrop, not a teleprompter.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG target stated. The deck renders in a browser at 1920×1080 with auto-scaling. Prefers-reduced-motion is wired up in styles.css. Font loading is CDN-dependent (Techbit + Google Fonts); falls back gracefully. Color contrast on body text should be verified — dark backgrounds with medium-opacity text are the primary risk.
